You are running domain names on the Internet using the deSEC DNS hosting platform. As part of your configuration, you already have set deSEC's nameservers for your domain (usually via your domain provider), so that the parent domain (e.g. .com) now directs DNS queries for your domain to our nameservers ("delegation").

We take security very seriously, and sign all your DNS data with DNSSEC automatically. Unfortunately, signing is only part of the story: clients also need to know which key to use for validation. This requires an additional step of configuration.

To complete your DNSSEC configuration, it is necessary to forward your domain's DNSSEC key data to your domain provider (just like you have configured the nameservers). Only once that is done, your domain's signatures can be validated.

To finish your domain's DNSSEC configuration, please log into the deSEC web portal, and click the (i) button next to your domain. The page will then display your domain's DNSSEC information, which you need to forward to your domain provider. (You can choose between two formats, DS and DNSKEY. It depends on your parent domain which format is needed, but your domain provider should tell you. For "DS" style, the shorter variant is preferred.)
